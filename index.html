Chicken Biryani

Chicken biryani is a delicious Pakistani/Indian rice dish that's typically reserved for special occasions such as weddings, parties, or holidays such as Ramadan. It has a lengthy preparation, but the work is definitely worth it. For biryani, basmati rice is the ideal variety to use.

Preparation time

  • Total:2-3 hours
  • Marinating 1 to 2 hrs
  • Cooking rice and meat 1 to 1.5 hrs

Ingredients:

  • 500g chicken (bone-in or boneless, cut into medium pieces)
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

Rice:

  • 2 cups basmati rice (soaked in water for 30 minutes)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • Salt to taste

For the Biryani:

  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 2 onions (thinly sliced)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves (chopped)
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon Biryani masala (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • Saffron strands soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons fried onions (for garnishing)
  • 2 tablespoons chopped cilantro and mint (for garnishing)
  • Ghee for layering

Instructions :

  1. Cook Rice:

    • Wash 1 Â½ cups of basmati rice and soak for 20 minutes.
    • Boil water in a pot, add 2 cardamom pods, 2-3 cloves, 1 cinnamon stick, and 1 bay leaf.
    • Cook the rice until 70% done (still a bit firm), then drain and set aside.

  2. Cook Chicken:

    • In a large pan, heat 2 tbsp oil or ghee.
    • Add sliced onions and fry until golden brown.
    • Add 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste, and 2 green chilies. Fry for a minute.
    • Add 300g chicken, 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp red chili powder, and salt. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the chicken is half-cooked.
    • Add 1 chopped tomato, 1/4 cup yogurt, and cook until the chicken is fully cooked and the oil separates.

  3. Layering:

    • In the same pan with the chicken, add chopped coriander and mint leaves.
    • Layer the half-cooked rice on top of the chicken evenly.
    • Sprinkle 1 tsp garam masala and some fried onions (if available).

  4. Dum (Steam Cooking):

    • Cover the pan with a tight lid. Cook on very low heat for 15-20 minutes (you can place a heavy pan on top of the lid for extra steam).
    • After 20 minutes, turn off the heat and let it sit for 5 minutes.

  5. Serve:

    • Gently mix the biryani, making sure to combine the rice and chicken evenly. Serve with raita or your favorite chutney.

Nutrition :

  • Calories: 400-450
  • Protein: 22-25g (from chicken)
  • Carbohydrates: 50-55g (mostly from rice)
  • Fat: 12-15g (from oil/ghee)
  • Fiber: 2-3g
  • Cholesterol: 70-90mg
  • Sodium: 700-800mg (depends on salt)
